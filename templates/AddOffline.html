About Franklin & Marshall

Franklin & Marshall College is a private, residential liberal arts college in Lancaster, Pennsylvania. Founded in 1787 and later renamed Franklin & Marshall after a merger in 1853, the college enrolls roughly 1,800–1,900 undergraduates and focuses exclusively on bachelor's-level education in the arts and sciences.

F&M is a coeducational liberal arts college awarding the Bachelor of Arts degree in more than 50 fields of study across the humanities, social sciences, natural sciences, and interdisciplinary areas.

  • Small classes with close faculty–student interaction and a strong emphasis on writing, discussion, and research.
  • All students are undergraduates, so faculty attention and resources are focused on them.
  • The motto is Lux et Lex (“Light and Law”).

Franklin & Marshall traces its roots to Franklin College, chartered in 1787 with support from Benjamin Franklin, and Marshall College, founded in 1836 and named for Chief Justice John Marshall. The two colleges merged in 1853 in Lancaster, creating Franklin & Marshall College.

Historic campus buildings such as Old Main, Goethean Hall, and Diagnothian Hall are listed on the National Register of Historic Places and reflect the college’s long history in American higher education.

F&M is located in Lancaster, Pennsylvania, a small city of about 60,000 people that serves as the hub of a larger county of more than half a million residents.

  • City–suburban campus of roughly 220 acres.
  • Easy train access to Philadelphia, Baltimore, and New York City.
  • Blend of historic architecture, green spaces, and nearby arts, food, and cultural scenes in downtown Lancaster.

F&M offers more than 50 fields of study, including traditional disciplines and interdisciplinary programs like Biological Foundations of Behavior and Scientific and Philosophical Studies of the Mind.

Students work closely with faculty, often in small seminars and advanced tutorials, and can design joint or individualized majors that cut across departments.

Undergraduate research and independent projects are a major part of the F&M experience. Students partner with faculty in the sciences, social sciences, and humanities on long-term investigations, fieldwork, and creative work.

  • Opportunities to present at conferences and co-author publications.
  • On-campus summer research programs and academic-year collaborations.
  • Mentored experiences that prepare students for graduate school, professional school, or research careers.

About half of F&M students participate in off-campus study, including study abroad, domestic programs, and field-based learning.

  • Programs across Europe, Latin America, Africa, Asia, and Oceania.
  • Special partnerships such as an advanced studies program in Bath, England.
  • Structured advising to connect programs with academic and professional goals.

Student Life & Outcomes

What it’s like to live and learn at F&M—and what comes next.

F&M is a residential college: most students live on campus in a system of College Houses and residence halls that anchor academic, social, and co-curricular life.

  • More than 100 student clubs and organizations, from cultural groups to performance, politics, and service.
  • Events on campus and in Lancaster, including arts, music, food, and community engagement.
  • A diverse student body representing many U.S. states and around 50 countries.

The F&M Diplomats compete mainly in NCAA Division III’s Centennial Conference, with Division I wrestling and nationally competitive squash.

  • Varsity, club, and intramural options for a wide range of sports and fitness interests.
  • Athletics are integrated into the broader campus culture rather than separated from academics.

F&M emphasizes outcomes and long-term success. The college reports strong retention and graduation rates and attracts students aiming for graduate school, professional school, and a wide range of careers.

  • High first-to-second-year retention (about 90%+) and competitive four- and six-year graduation rates.
  • Ranked among top national liberal arts colleges and “Best Value” schools by several publications.
  • Alumni paths include research, healthcare, law, business, education, nonprofit work, and more.

F&M aims to combine rigorous academics, a close-knit residential community, and strong mentoring so that students can “achieve beyond what they themselves thought possible.”
